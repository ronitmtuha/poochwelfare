ABOUT US

THE POOCH WELFARE FOUNDATION was started as a STUDENT VOLUNTEER FOUNDATION in May, 2019. However, we registered as a trust with the Charity Commissioner, Pune in February 2021.

We envision a world in which the practice of kindness and compassion is taught and encouraged at every level of our society, guaranteeing fair and humane treatment for the animals.

We strive to provide dignity to animals, who are brutally and viciously injured, & suffering from chronic diseases and allow them to live out their lives at the shelter in a safe & peaceful environment.

We also carry out mass Anti Rabbies Vaccination in order to promote a Human-Animal Friendly behaviour

Apart from rescue we also provide adoptions, animal cruelty prevention and control and volunteering opportunities.

We are currently focused more on stray cats and dogs, and soon we will be focusing on other animals too, as our community increases and is capable of handling the same.

WHAT DO WE DO?

ANIMAL ADOPTION

The number of euthanised strays could be reduced dramatically if more people adopted, instead of buying them. When you adopt, you save a loving animal by making them part of your family and open up shelter space for one who might desperately need it. The Pooch Welfare Foundation gets the strays adopted with full background check of the adopter, the family, the financial condition and other necessary verifications. They’re only handed over to good and capable households only after all formal processes. We do it absolutely FREE of cost.
“ADOPT, DON'T SHOP!”

ANTI RABBLIES DRIVE VACCINATION DRIVE

A bite from an animal with rabies to a human can cause death as currently there is no effective treatment for Rabies. To avoid this we give most of the animals Anti Rabies in order to promote a human-animal friendly behavior The Pooch Welfare Foundation has taken an initiative to vaccinate the all most all pooches with anti rabies vaccination. Feel Free to Contact us to sponsor a anti rabies drive from your end.

ANIMAL BIRTH CONTROL - STERLIZATION DRIVE

The single most important thing that we can do to save cats and dogs from all the suffering and death that their overpopulation causes is to spay and neuter them. The only ethical and the most effective way of managing a street dog population is through programs. The Pooch Welfare Foundation has sterilized and vaccinated over 100 + stray dogs and cats so far.

ANIMAL RESCUE - TREATING INJURED ANIMALS

The Pooch Welfare Foundation is founded by student volunteers who felt the need to build a free of cost medical facility for the stray animals where the best, ethical and highly skilled medical care is readily made available. We try to give proper medical treatment to our pooches, thru most of the vets and animal hospital we have tied up with in Pune.

REFLECTIVE COLLAR DRIVE

reflective collar is tied on the neck of the animals and it is visible from a long distance, therefore it makes the drivers aware that there is an animal on the road especially in the night so that the driver can slow down the vehicle preventing the road accident. As most if the animals are injured through car accidents, the Pooch Welfare Foundation has started the reflective collar drive. We’ve started putting them on our pooch’s, especially on the streets which are prone to accidents.

VOLUNTEER SERVICES

When it comes to feeding strays, consistency is important.That’s why we have volunteers all over the city who feed the pooch’s who live in their area, regularly! We also have volunteers who volunteer to help in the cases, they help in taking the animal to the veterinary doctor for the treatment. The other volunteers help in coordination of fosters and clinic, social media, funding etc.
